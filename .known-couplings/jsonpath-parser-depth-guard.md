# JSONPath parser depth guard must cover every recursive production

`_JsonPathParser` (`packages/json/_json_path_parser.pony`) keeps its native-stack recursion bounded by incrementing a shared `_depth` counter at three points — `_parse_paren_expr`, `_parse_function_call`, and `_parse_filter_selector` — and failing with a catchable `JsonPathParseError` once it passes `_JsonPathMaxDepth`. This works only because every recursion cycle in the filter grammar passes through one of those three productions once per nesting level: parentheses and `!(...)` go through `_parse_paren_expr`, nested value-functions (`length(length(...))`) through `_parse_function_call`, and nested `[?...]` filters through `_parse_filter_selector`. Operator chains (`&&`/`||`) and dot/segment chains are parsed in loops, not recursion, so they need no guard here.

If you add a production to the filter grammar that can recurse — directly or mutually — without passing through one of the three guarded functions, a crafted query string can again drive the parser's native stack into the ground (an uncatchable SIGSEGV), the crash issue #5606 fixed. Wrap the new recursion in an `_enter()?`/`_leave()` pair, or route it through an already-guarded production. The same bound also protects evaluation: `_FilterEval`/`_JsonPathEval` recurse on the parse tree's nesting depth, and capping that depth at parse time caps it at eval time too. The guarded points carry inline comments pointing back here.

Two subtleties. First, `_parse_test_or_comparison` backtracks inside a `try` that can enter a guarded production and swallow its error, so it saves and restores `_depth` alongside the offset rewind — a new error-swallowing `try` around a guarded call needs the same save/restore or the counter drifts. Second, the eval-time bound depends on every `JsonPath` coming through this capped parser: the evaluator's nested-filter/function recursion is still native (it is bounded only by the cap, not by a work stack of its own), and `JsonPath`'s constructor is private precisely so the parser is the only source. A future public/programmatic `JsonPath` builder that skipped the parser would re-open the eval-time overflow with no cap to catch it; the comment on `JsonPath._create` flags this.

Run: `make test-stdlib-debug test-stdlib-release` (the json suite includes the `json/jsonpath/parse/depth-limit`, `.../depth-boundary`, `json/jsonpath/filter/chain-stack-safe`, and `json/jsonpath/filter/nested-eval-stack-safe` regression tests).
